Ocean Habitat

Photo by Shutterstock

An ocean habitat covers more than half of Earth's surface and these salty waters are a home to many creatures and plants.

Mola

Picture taken by Adrian Kaye

Here is a quote from National Geographic:

The mola has an unusual look. It's round. Its tail fin doesn't grow along with the rest of the fish after the mola hatches.
It remains short and rounded, adding to the fish's overall round shape.
The short fin is called a clavus. Molas use the clavus to steer as they swim.
Molas enjoy sunning themselves at the surface of the ocean. This is why they are also called sunfish.
