Freshwater Habitat

Picture taken by Getty Images

Freshwater habitats include rivers, creeks, lakes, ponds, streams, and wetlands (such as swamps and marshes). Freshwater accounts for only three percent of the world's water.

Red-Bellied Piranha

Picture taken by Prillfoto, Dreamstime

Here is a quote from National Geographic:

Red-bellied piranhas live in lakes and rivers in South America. They sometimes use their mighty mouths to gobble up other fish or animals such as shrimp, worms, and mollusks.
They swim and hunt in shoals, or schools, that can include more than a hundred individuals.
According to legend, the animals, which can grow about 14 inches long, have even devoured human swimmers.

American Bullfrog

Picture taken by Joel Sartore

Here is a quote from National Geographic:

The male bullfrog's call is deep and loud. Jug-o-rum, jug-o-rum, it calls. Some people think it sounds like a cow mooing, which is why the frog has "bull" in its name.
Bullfrogs eat all kinds of insects, mice, snakes, fish, and other small creatures. They hunt at night, waiting patiently until they see something pass by that they figure would make a good meal.
Bullfrogs like warm weather. When it turns cold, they dig down into mud to hibernate.
